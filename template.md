## Context

What is the situation that requires a decision? Two or three short paragraphs.
Cover the relevant facts, constraints, and forces at play — not what you decided,
just what you were looking at. Anyone reading this in six months should
understand *why this came up* without needing the surrounding conversation.

If the decision was prompted by something specific (a cost surprise, a lab
exercise, an exam topic, a piece of reading), say so. Concrete triggers are
easier to remember than abstract motivations.

## Decision

State the decision in one or two sentences, in the active voice.

> We will [do X] using [approach Y], because [primary reason].

Then, if useful, a short paragraph elaborating on what exactly was decided —
the specific service, configuration, or pattern. Keep this tight; the reasoning
belongs in the next section.

## Alternatives Considered

note the main reason it wasn't selected. Two to four alternatives is the
sweet spot — fewer suggests you didn't look around, more suggests you're
inventing options to look thorough.
